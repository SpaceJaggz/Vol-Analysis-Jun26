14 / Good news and the gap

What the spike validated, and what it exposed

Good news

  • Whales are sticky. 100% of $1M+ wallets stayed active, generating $21.6M more in the 17 days that followed.
  • The volume is real. Monetised at the full 6 bps take rate across spike and normal days.
  • Pear traders won the move. ~0% liquidation share. They traded the volatility, not got worked by it.
  • Mid-tier sticks too. 94% retention, with post-spike volume actually higher than during the spike.

To improve

  • 67% in 5 wallets. Real concentration risk. If one or two rotate out, a meaningful chunk of volume follows.
  • Small tier churns at 32%. The acquisition funnel is leakier than the whale tier.
  • Rebate exists but is manual. A live incentive lever that doesn't show in surface metrics. Worth considering whether to lean on it more explicitly.
  • Auto-rebalance still idle. Volume engine sitting at zero today. Launching well removes some dependency on event-driven days.
15 / Takeaways

What to take away

  1. It was a volatility event, not growth. Existing whales, broad asset participation, negligible new-user volume. Do not bank it as a new baseline.
  2. Bigger trades beat more traders. Size per trader rose 77% versus 50% more participants. Depth scales with whale conviction.
  3. The volume stuck. 100% whale retention 17 days out, monetised at full take rate, ~0% liquidations. The spike was earned and kept.
  4. Concentrated in 5 wallets. 67% of spike volume. Knowing and keeping them matters more than headcount growth.
  5. Auto-rebalance is dormant volume. A controllable lever sitting idle. Once live, it adds a baseline that doesn't need a crash.
16 / What we can control

Levers worth considering

We can't manufacture volatility. We can decide who shows up when it arrives, and we can stage volume around catalysts that are already on the calendar.

AOwn the top wallets
5 wallets = 67% of spike volume, 100% retained post-spike. The most valuable asset already exists.
  • Identify the top ~20 wallets and understand why they trade Pear
  • Direct line for incidents and feature requests — treat them as design partners
  • Manual rebate system already in place — worth reviewing whether to surface it more visibly to qualifying wallets
  • Open question: does this top-5 composition hold in 3 months, or is a pipeline needed?
BCalendar campaigns around volatility catalysts
Volatility is the trigger. Many catalysts are pre-scheduled. Timing is the lever we control.
Catalysts already on the calendar
  • FOMC (8×/year, dates published) · CPI · PCE · NFP (monthly Fridays)
  • Crypto options expiry (Deribit, end-of-quarter especially)
  • Token unlocks · big-cap earnings (for RWA equity pairs) · halving windows
Campaign formats to overlay
  • 24–48h volume sprint around the event, tiered prize pool (top 10 / top 50 / all qualifiers)
  • Pair-specific bonus matched to catalyst (FOMC → BTC/ETH, NFP → USD pairs, earnings → equity pairs)
  • Proactive Agent Pear nudge: "FOMC in 2 hours — set up your pair"
T&C sketch (for discussion)
  • Eligibility: PEARPROTOCOLUI or AGENTPEAR only — keeps it organic, excludes API/Bullpen
  • Window: opening and closing times fixed in UTC, pinned in announcement
  • Minimum: e.g. $10K notional to qualify, filters dust
  • Anti-gaming: exclude self-cross wash trades, minimum hold time or notional × time-held weighting
Developer needs (rough)
  • Real-time leaderboard per campaign (fills + cloid filter)
  • Pre-event push via Agent Pear (cron trigger keyed to calendar)
  • Snapshot + automated payout at end-of-window
  • Post-campaign lift dashboard — measures each event vs prior baseline so we learn what to repeat
